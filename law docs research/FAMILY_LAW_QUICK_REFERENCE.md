## Legal Test Cheat Sheet

### s60CC BEST INTERESTS TEST

**Mandatory Structure:**
1. Consider PRIMARY considerations first (s60CC(2))
2. Consider ADDITIONAL considerations (s60CC(3))
3. Give most weight to PRIMARY

**PRIMARY CONSIDERATIONS (Must consider both):**
- **(a) Meaningful Relationship:** Benefit of meaningful relationship with both parents
- **(b) Protection from Harm:** Need to protect from abuse, neglect, family violence

**If conflict â†’ Protection from Harm WINS**

**KEY ADDITIONAL CONSIDERATIONS:**
- (a) Views of child
- (c) Willingness to facilitate relationship with other parent
- (d) Likely effect of changes
- (f) Capacity to provide for child's needs
- (h) Family violence (if applicable)

**Detection Keywords:**
```
best interests, child welfare, meaningful relationship, safety,
harm, risk, views of child, capacity, family violence
```

---

### s79 PROPERTY SETTLEMENT (Four-Step Approach)

**STEP 1: Identify & Value Pool**
- What property exists?
- What is net value (assets - liabilities)?

**STEP 2: Assess Contributions (s79(4))**
- (a) Financial contributions
- (b) Non-financial contributions
- (c) Homemaker/parent contributions

Result: Percentage split (e.g., 60/40)

**STEP 3: Assess Future Needs (s75(2))**
- Age and health
- Income and earning capacity
- Care of children under 18
- Duration of marriage/relationship
- Standard of living

Result: Adjustment (typically 0-20%)

**STEP 4: Just & Equitable?**
- Is the final percentage fair?
- Any supervening circumstances?

**Key Case:** *Hickey v Hickey* [2003] FamCA 395

**Detection Keywords:**
```
property settlement, contributions, financial, non-financial,
homemaker, parent, future needs, just and equitable, asset pool
```

## Special Features

### Anonymization (s121)
- **ALL reported cases use pseudonyms**
- Common formats: [ABC] and [XYZ], Mr Smith/Mrs Smith
- Criminal offence to publish identifying details

### Closed Court (s97)
- Proceedings closed to public
- Only parties, lawyers, ICL, court staff attend

### Independent Children's Lawyer (s68LA)
- Appointed in 16% of parenting cases
- Represents child's best interests (not child's instructions)
- Most common in high-conflict or family violence cases

### Less Adversarial Trial
- All parenting proceedings since 2006
- Judicial control of questioning
- Reduced cross-examination
- Family reports admitted as evidence

### "Can I get equal time with my children?"
**Routes to:**
- s61DA (presumption of ESPR)
- s65DAA (must consider equal time if ESPR applies)
- s60CC (best interests paramount)

**Key Questions:**
- Is ESPR presumption rebutted? (family violence?)
- Is equal time reasonably practicable?
- Is equal time in child's best interests?

- Distance/logistics considerations

### "How will property be divided?"
**Routes to:**
- s79 power
- Hickey v Hickey four-step approach
- Stanford v Stanford

**Key Questions:**
- What is the asset pool value?
- What were each party's contributions (financial, non-financial, homemaker)?
- What are future needs factors (age, health, care of children)?

**System provides:**

### "What is family violence and how does it affect my case?"
**Routes to:**
- s4AB definition
- s60CC(2)(b) and (3)(h) best interests factors
- MRR v GR principles

**Key Concepts:**
- Family violence = coercion, control, causing fear
- Broad definition (includes psychological, economic, social)
- Safety is paramount in parenting matters
- May rebut ESPR presumption

**System identifies:**
- Family violence case law
- Protection mechanisms (supervised time, ICL)
