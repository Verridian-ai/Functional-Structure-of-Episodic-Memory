## 6. Practical Application Guide

### 6.1 Document Classification Workflow

#### Step 1: Identify Primary Domain
**Question**: Is this document primarily about commercial/business law?
- **Yes** → Proceed to KL classification
- **No** → Check other domains (KP, KB, KE, KT, KD, etc.)

#### Step 2: Identify Subcategory
**Questions**:
1. Is it about **contracts**? → KL100
2. Is it about **consumer protection**? → KL200
3. Is it about **corporations/company law**? → KL300
4. Is it about **competition/antitrust**? → KL400
5. Is it about **insolvency**? → KL500
6. Is it about **banking/finance**? → KL600
7. Is it about **intellectual property**? → KL700
8. Is it about **international trade**? → KL800

#### Step 3: Check for Specific Topics
Within each subcategory, identify the specific topic code (e.g., KL301, KL302).

#### Step 4: Extract Keywords
Scan document for classification keywords (Section 5) to confirm classification.

### 6.2 Common Classification Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: Contract Dispute Case
**Document**: Case about breach of supply agreement
- **Primary Classification**: KL100 (Contracts)
- **Specific Topic**: KL106 (Performance and discharge) or KL107 (Breach and remedies)
- **Keywords**: Breach of contract, damages, specific performance

#### Scenario 2: ASIC Enforcement Action
**Document**: ASIC v Company regarding misleading prospectus
- **Primary Classification**: KL300 (Corporations)
- **Specific Topic**: KL311 (Corporate fundraising and disclosure)
- **Keywords**: Prospectus, misleading disclosure, ASIC enforcement, s1041H

#### Scenario 3: Consumer Refund Claim
**Document**: Consumer seeking refund for defective product
- **Primary Classification**: KL200 (Consumer Law)
- **Specific Topic**: KL205 (Consumer guarantees)
- **Keywords**: Acceptable quality, s54 ACL, consumer remedies, refund rights

#### Scenario 4: Merger Clearance
**Document**: ACCC merger clearance application
- **Primary Classification**: KL400 (Competition Law)
- **Specific Topic**: KL408 (Mergers and acquisitions)
- **Keywords**: s50, SLC test, merger clearance, market definition, ACCC

#### Scenario 5: Liquidation Proceedings
**Document**: Winding up application and unfair preference claim
- **Primary Classification**: KL500 (Insolvency)
- **Specific Topics**: KL509 (Court-ordered winding up), KL513 (Unfair preferences)
- **Keywords**: s459P, s588FA, liquidation, voidable transactions

#### Scenario 6: PPSA Security Interest
**Document**: Priority dispute over secured assets
- **Primary Classification**: KL600 (Banking & Finance)
- **Specific Topic**: KL605 (Security interests and mortgages)
- **Keywords**: PPSA, perfection, priority rules, s55, PMSI

#### Scenario 7: Trade Mark Opposition
**Document**: Opposition to trade mark application
- **Primary Classification**: KL700 (Intellectual Property)
- **Specific Topic**: KL706 (Trade mark registration and use)
- **Keywords**: s44, deceptive similarity, distinctive character, opposition

### 6.3 Multi-Domain Documents

**Some documents span multiple domains**:

#### Example 1: Insolvency + Consumer Law
**Document**: Liquidator pursuing s588G claim for insolvent trading while customers claim consumer guarantees
- **Primary**: KL500 (Insolvency) - if focus is on insolvent trading
- **Secondary**: KL200 (Consumer Law) - if consumer claims are significant
- **Approach**: Use primary classification based on main legal issue

#### Example 2: Corporations + Competition
**Document**: Merger involving listed companies with ACCC review
- **Primary**: KL400 (Competition) - if focus is on merger clearance
- **Secondary**: KL300 (Corporations) - if focus is on takeover process
- **Approach**: Classify based on primary legal question

#### Example 3: Contract + Consumer Law
**Document**: Standard form contract with unfair terms
- **Primary**: KL200 (Consumer Law) - if focus is on ACL s23 unfair terms
- **Alternative**: KL100 (Contracts) - if focus is on general contract law
- **Approach**: Consumer protection issues generally take precedence
