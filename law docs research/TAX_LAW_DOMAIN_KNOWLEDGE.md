# Tax Law Domain Knowledge

## Overview
This document provides comprehensive domain knowledge for Australian Tax Law, covering federal and state taxation systems, key legislation, legal concepts, and classification frameworks.

---

## 1. Key Legislation

### Federal Tax Legislation

#### Income Tax Assessment Act 1936 (Cth) (ITAA 1936)
- Original income tax legislation (being progressively replaced)
- Part IVA: General anti-avoidance provisions
- Division 6: Trust income taxation
- Division 7A: Deemed dividends from private companies
- Sections on international tax arrangements
- Continues to contain foundational provisions

#### Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (Cth) (ITAA 1997)
- Modern rewrite of income tax law
- Division 6: Assessable income
- Division 8: Deductions
- Division 40: Capital allowances and depreciation
- Division 115: Capital gains tax discount
- Division 328: Small business entities
- Division 855: Foreign residents and CGT
- Divisions 900-915: Substantiation rules

#### A New Tax System (Goods and Services Tax) Act 1999 (Cth)
- GST registration and liability
- Taxable supplies and taxable importations
- Input tax credits
- GST-free supplies (food, health, education)
- Input-taxed supplies (financial services, residential rent)
- Adjustment provisions
- Special rules for different industries

#### Fringe Benefits Tax Assessment Act 1986 (Cth) (FBTAA)
- Car fringe benefits
- Loan fringe benefits
- Expense payment fringe benefits
- Housing fringe benefits
- Living-away-from-home allowances
- Entertainment benefits
- Otherwise deductible rule
- Reportable fringe benefits

#### Taxation Administration Act 1953 (Cth) (TAA)
- Pay As You Go (PAYG) withholding
- PAYG instalments
- Collection and recovery provisions
- Penalties and interest
- General interest charge (GIC)
- Shortfall interest charge (SIC)
- Objections and appeals process
- Record-keeping requirements

#### Other Federal Tax Legislation
- **Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Act 1993**: Superannuation fund regulation
- **Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992**: Employer superannuation obligations
- **Petroleum Resource Rent Tax Assessment Act 1987**: Resource taxation
- **Excise Acts**: Fuel, alcohol, tobacco excise
- **Customs Act 1901**: Import duties and customs tariffs
- **Tax Agent Services Act 2009**: Regulation of tax practitioners

### State Revenue Legislation

#### New South Wales
- **Duties Act 1997 (NSW)**: Transfer duty (stamp duty)
- **Land Tax Management Act 1956 (NSW)**: Land tax
- **Payroll Tax Act 2007 (NSW)**: Payroll tax

#### Victoria
- **Duties Act 2000 (Vic)**: Stamp duty
- **Land Tax Act 2005 (Vic)**: Land tax
- **Payroll Tax Act 2007 (Vic)**: Payroll tax

#### Queensland
- **Duties Act 2001 (Qld)**: Transfer duty
- **Land Tax Act 2010 (Qld)**: Land tax
- **Payroll Tax Act 1971 (Qld)**: Payroll tax

#### Other States and Territories
- Similar legislative frameworks in WA, SA, TAS, NT, ACT
- Harmonisation efforts through model legislation
- Variations in rates and thresholds

## 3. Key Legal Concepts & Keywords

### 3.1 Income Tax Concepts

#### Assessable Income
- **Ordinary income** (s 6-5 ITAA 1997): Income according to ordinary concepts
- **Statutory income** (s 6-10 ITAA 1997): Amounts specifically included by legislation
- **Gross income vs net income**: Distinction for various provisions
- **Derivation**: When income is derived (cash vs accruals basis)
- **Source of income**: Australian source vs foreign source
- **Capital vs revenue**: Fundamental distinction for taxation

#### Allowable Deductions
- **General deductions** (s 8-1 ITAA 1997): Losses or outgoings incurred in gaining assessable income
- **Nexus test**: Connection between expenditure and income
- **Capital vs revenue expenditure**: Only revenue deductible under s 8-1
- **Negative limbs**: Private/domestic, capital, penalties not deductible
- **Specific deductions**: Sections 25-25 to 25-115 for particular expenses
- **Timing of deductions**: Prepayments, economic performance
- **Substantiation**: Record-keeping requirements for claims

#### Taxable Income Calculation
- **Assessable income** minus **deductions** equals **taxable income**
- **Net capital gain**: Included in assessable income
- **Tax offsets**: Reduce tax payable (not taxable income)
- **Medicare levy**: Calculated on taxable income
- **Tax-free threshold**: $18,200 for individuals

### 3.2 Anti-Avoidance

#### Part IVA (General Anti-Avoidance)
- **Tax benefit**: Reduction in tax liability or increase in loss
- **Scheme**: Arrangement or plan
- **Dominant purpose test**: Would reasonable person conclude dominant purpose was tax benefit?
- **Objective test**: Not based on taxpayer's subjective purpose
- **Predication test**: Not merely incidental or by-product
- **Commissioner's discretion**: Cancel tax benefit
- **Penalties**: 50% of tax shortfall for schemes

#### Specific Anti-Avoidance Rules
- **Division 7A**: Deemed dividends from private companies (loans, payments, debt forgiveness)
- **Thin capitalisation**: Limits debt deductions for multinational entities
- **Transfer pricing**: Ensures arm's length dealings between related parties
- **Loss duplication**: Prevents double deduction on sale of assets
- **CGT wash sales**: Prevention of artificial capital losses
- **Value shifting**: Prevents shifting value between assets tax-free

#### Tax Avoidance vs Tax Evasion
- **Tax avoidance**: Legal arrangement to minimize tax (may be caught by Part IVA)
- **Tax evasion**: Illegal concealment or misrepresentation
- **Tax planning**: Legitimate structuring within law
- **Aggressive tax planning**: May attract Part IVA or specific anti-avoidance rules

### 3.3 Capital Gains Tax

#### CGT Events
- **Disposal**: Sale, gift, loss, destruction, ending of asset
- **Timing**: When CGT event happens (usually contract date)
- **Capital proceeds**: Money or property received for CGT event
- **Active asset**: Used in business (relevant for small business concessions)

#### CGT Discount
- **50% discount**: For individuals and trusts (not companies)
- **12-month holding period**: Must own asset for at least 12 months
- **Discount capital gain**: Capital gain after applying discount
- **Net capital gain**: After offsetting capital losses and applying discount

#### Small Business CGT Concessions
- **$6 million net asset value test**: Business and connected entities
- **Active asset test**: Asset used in business for at least half of ownership period
- **15-year exemption**: No CGT if owned 15+ years and retiring
- **50% active asset reduction**: Reduces capital gain by 50%
- **Retirement exemption**: Up to $500,000 lifetime limit
- **Rollover**: Defer CGT on replacement or similar business assets

### 3.4 GST Concepts

#### Input Tax Credits
- **Creditable acquisition**: Acquisition for business purpose, GST-registered, price includes GST
- **Input tax credit entitlement**: GST included in price paid
- **Non-deductible expenses**: No input tax credits (e.g., entertainment, private use)
- **Reducing input tax credit entitlement**: Acquisitions partly for non-business purpose

#### Taxable Supply
- **Supply for consideration**: Provided for payment
- **In course or furtherance of enterprise**: Business activity
- **Connected with Australia**: Made in Australia or involves Australian property
- **GST-free or input-taxed**: Special categories that are not taxable supplies

#### Adjustments
- **Increasing adjustments**: Owe more GST (e.g., bad debt recovery, change in creditable purpose)
- **Decreasing adjustments**: Owe less GST (e.g., price reduction, bad debts)
- **Adjustment events**: Changes to consideration or creditable purpose

### 3.5 Fringe Benefits Tax

#### Taxable Value
- **Grossed-up taxable value**: Type 1 (2.0802) and Type 2 (1.8868) for FBT year 2024
- **Otherwise deductible rule**: Reduces taxable value if employee could have claimed deduction
- **Employee contribution**: Reduces taxable value dollar-for-dollar

#### FBT Tax Rate
- **47%**: Current FBT rate
- **Employer pays**: FBT is paid by employer, not employee
- **Grossing-up**: Reflects pre-tax equivalent value

### 3.6 Transfer Pricing

#### Arm's Length Principle
- **Independent parties**: Dealings between unrelated parties
- **Commercial reality**: What would have occurred in open market
- **Reconstruction**: ATO can reconstruct conditions to arm's length
- **Comparable uncontrolled price**: Comparison with similar transactions

#### Documentation
- **Master file**: Group-wide information
- **Local file**: Australian entity specific information
- **Country-by-country report**: For significant global entities
- **Reasonable efforts**: Penalty protection for contemporaneous documentation

### 3.7 Interpretation Principles

#### Statutory Interpretation
- **Literal approach**: Plain meaning of words
- **Purposive approach**: Purpose and policy behind legislation
- **Context**: Reading provisions in context of entire Act
- **Extrinsic materials**: Explanatory memoranda, second reading speeches

#### Common Law Principles
- **Westrader principle**: Economic substance over legal form (limited application)
- **Beneficial ownership**: Substance over form for asset ownership
- **Sham doctrine**: Transactions without genuine commercial effect
- **Fiscal nullity**: Transactions that cancel each other out

## 4. Courts & Tribunals

### 4.1 Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) - Taxation Division

#### Jurisdiction
- Reviews decisions made by ATO Commissioner
- Merit review (reconsiders facts and law)
- Primary avenue for disputing tax assessments
- Can affirm, vary, or set aside ATO decisions

#### Procedure
- Application for review within 60 days of objection decision
- Lodgement fee (refundable if successful)
- Informal and less expensive than courts
- Can request reasons for decision
- Evidence rules more flexible than courts

#### Appeals from AAT
- Appeal to Federal Court on questions of law only
- Cannot appeal on questions of fact
- Must obtain leave to appeal in some cases

### 4.2 Federal Court of Australia

#### Original Jurisdiction
- Appeals from AAT on questions of law
- Direct appeals from Commissioner's objection decisions (bypassing AAT)
- Recovery proceedings by Commissioner
- Injunctions to prevent tax schemes

#### Procedure
- Federal Court Rules apply
- More formal and expensive than AAT
- Evidence rules strictly applied
- Written and oral submissions
- Typically single judge at first instance

#### Case Management
- Practice Note TAX 1: Case management of taxation matters
- Mediation and alternative dispute resolution encouraged
- Disclosure obligations
- Expert evidence requirements

### 4.3 Full Federal Court

#### Jurisdiction
- Appeals from single Federal Court judges
- Hears appeals on questions of law
- Can consider factual findings if palpable error
- Three-judge bench (occasionally five judges)

#### Precedent Value
- Binding on single Federal Court judges
- Highly persuasive for AAT
- Resolved conflicts between single judge decisions
- Important precedent for tax law development

### 4.4 High Court of Australia

#### Jurisdiction
- Final court of appeal in Australian hierarchy
- Appeals from Full Federal Court
- Requires special leave to appeal (granted sparingly)
- Considers matters of public importance or legal principle

#### Procedure
- Application for special leave
- Test: Is issue of public importance or significant legal principle?
- Limited number of tax cases heard annually
- Highest precedent value

#### Landmark Tax Cases
- *Federal Commissioner of Taxation v Spotless Services Ltd* (1996): Interpretation of "incurred"
- *Commissioner of Taxation v Hart* (2004): Part IVA analysis
- *Hepples v Federal Commissioner of Taxation* (1991): Deductibility test
- *Federal Commissioner of Taxation v Consolidated Media Holdings Ltd* (2012): Part IVA dominant purpose
- *Commissioner of Taxation v Bargwanna* (2012): Reasonably incidental business purpose
- *Bamford v Commissioner of Taxation* (2010): Trust income and present entitlement

### 4.5 State Courts and Tribunals

#### State Supreme Courts
- State tax matters (payroll tax, land tax, stamp duty)
- Appeals from state revenue tribunals
- Judicial review of administrative decisions

#### State Revenue Tribunals
- Review of state tax assessments
- Civil and Administrative Tribunals (e.g., NCAT in NSW, VCAT in Victoria)
- Merit review similar to AAT

## 5. ATO Rulings and Guidance

### 5.1 Public Rulings

#### Taxation Rulings (TR)
- **TR series**: Comprehensive interpretation of tax law
- **Binding on Commissioner**: ATO must apply consistently
- **Taxpayer reliance**: Can rely on published ruling
- **Precedential value**: Considered by AAT and courts (but not binding)
- **Examples**: TR 2006/10 (CGT and main residence), TR 2013/3 (deductions for income-producing activities)

#### Goods and Services Tax Rulings (GSTR)
- **GST interpretation**: Application of GST law
- **Industry-specific guidance**: Particular sectors (financial services, property, etc.)
- **Examples**: GSTR 2000/19 (tax invoices), GSTR 2006/9 (margin scheme)

#### Legislative Instruments
- **Binding legal force**: Made under legislative power
- **Class rulings**: Apply to specific class of taxpayers or arrangements
- **Product rulings**: Pre-arranged products (e.g., managed funds)

### 5.2 Private Rulings

#### Individual Rulings
- **Written advice**: Specific to taxpayer's circumstances
- **Binding protection**: Commissioner bound by ruling for that taxpayer
- **Application process**: Lodge application with specific facts
- **Fee**: May apply for complex rulings
- **Objection rights**: Can object if unfavorable ruling

#### Oral Rulings
- **Phone or in-person advice**: Less formal
- **Limited binding effect**: Only in specified circumstances
- **Documented**: ATO records oral rulings
- **Simple matters**: Straightforward factual application

### 5.3 Practice Statements

#### Law Administration Practice Statements (PS LA)
- **ATO administration**: How ATO applies and administers law
- **Not law interpretation**: Describes ATO practice
- **Examples**: PS LA 2011/27 (objections and appeals), PS LA 2012/5 (advance pricing arrangements)

#### General Administration Practice Statements (PS GA)
- **Administrative processes**: Internal ATO procedures
- **Transparency**: How ATO makes decisions

### 5.4 Practical Compliance Guidelines (PCG)

#### Purpose
- **Compliance approach**: ATO's risk assessment framework
- **Safe harbours**: Low-risk zones for taxpayers
- **Risk assessment**: Green, amber, red zones

#### Key PCGs
- **PCG 2017/D12**: Director identification number
- **PCG 2021/4**: Allocation of professional firm profits
- **PCG 2023/1**: Work test exemption
- **PCG 2018/9**: Central management and control test for residency

### 5.5 Taxpayer Alerts (TA)

#### Warning Notices
- **Emerging schemes**: New tax avoidance arrangements
- **No ruling**: Describe concern without final position
- **ATO scrutiny**: Transactions will be closely examined
- **Examples**: TA 2023/1 (CGT rollovers), TA 2022/2 (trust vesting)

### 5.6 Determinations

#### Taxation Determinations (TD)
- **Brief rulings**: Short answers to specific questions
- **Binding**: Commissioner bound by determinations
- **Quick reference**: Practical guidance
- **Examples**: TD 2022/8 (private company SBE indicators), TD 2021/8 (superannuation guarantee)

#### Industry-Specific
- **Product Determinations**: Specific financial products
- **Class Rulings**: Particular arrangements or transactions

### 5.7 ATO Guidance Materials

#### Fact Sheets and Guides
- **Plain English explanations**: Not legally binding
- **Taxpayer education**: Broad guidance
- **Examples**: "Tax basics for small business", "Guide to capital gains tax"

#### ATO Website
- **Online resources**: Tax time toolkit, calculators
- **myTax guidance**: Help for individual tax returns
- **Business portal**: Resources for business taxpayers

#### ATO Community
- **Professional forums**: For tax agents and advisers
- **Technical discussions**: Emerging issues
- **Consultation**: Draft rulings and legislation

## 7. Research and Analysis Framework

### Case Law Research
- **Identify relevant legislation**: Start with statutory provisions
- **Find leading cases**: Use AustLII, LexisNexis, CCH
- **Trace judicial history**: Note appeals and subsequent treatment
- **Consider tribunal decisions**: AAT decisions on similar facts
- **Review commentary**: Tax journals, textbooks, professional publications

### Legislative Interpretation
- **Read explanatory memoranda**: Parliamentary intent and purpose
- **Check amendments**: Historical changes to provisions
- **Review ATO guidance**: Public rulings and determinations
- **Consider policy**: Underlying tax policy objectives
- **Apply principles**: Statutory interpretation principles

### Practical Application
- **Gather facts**: Complete and accurate factual matrix
- **Identify issues**: Legal and factual questions
- **Apply law to facts**: Reasoning from principle to conclusion
- **Consider alternatives**: Different structures or approaches
- **Assess risks**: Likelihood of ATO challenge or audit
- **Document advice**: Clear reasoning and assumptions

## 8. Key Tax Principles

### Fundamental Principles
1. **Worldwide income**: Residents taxed on worldwide income
2. **Source-based taxation**: Non-residents taxed on Australian-source income
3. **Ability to pay**: Progressive tax rates reflect capacity
4. **Equity**: Horizontal (similar taxpayers) and vertical (different capacities)
5. **Efficiency**: Minimal distortion to economic decisions
6. **Simplicity**: Understandable and administrable
7. **Certainty**: Clear rules and predictable outcomes

### Tax Policy Objectives
- **Revenue raising**: Fund government services
- **Redistribution**: Progressive taxation and welfare
- **Economic management**: Fiscal policy tool
- **Behavior modification**: Encourage/discourage activities (e.g., R&D incentives)
- **Social objectives**: Support for families, education, retirement

### Compliance Philosophy
- **Self-assessment**: Taxpayers calculate own tax
- **Voluntary compliance**: Most taxpayers comply willingly
- **Risk-based administration**: ATO focuses on high-risk areas
- **Justified trust**: Assume compliance unless contrary indicators
- **Proportionate response**: Penalties match seriousness
